Timeline

1917

TOTO is founded. Japan’s modern toilet revolution begins with a mission to improve public hygiene.

1980

The Washlet is born. TOTO’s first electronic bidet seat changes bathroom culture forever.

1993

Heated seats become a household norm. Washlets start drawing global attention.

2002

Smart toilets roll out: motion sensors, automatic lids, and memory features enter homes.

2007

Eco-flush tech reduces water use. Washlets lead Japan’s sustainable design push.

2025

Washlets go global. Once niche, now they’re in luxury homes across the world.

Washlets was actually inspired by Westerner's design and technology

Before the tech, traditional squat toilets were the norm. They were called Washikis 和式. In 1903, Kazuchika Okura saw ceramic flush toilets in Europe. Back home, Japan still used squat toilets with no plumbing. By 1914, he made Japan’s first Western-style toilet — and founded TOTO soon after!
